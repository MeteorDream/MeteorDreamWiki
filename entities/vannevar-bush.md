# Vannevar Bush

American engineer whose 1945 essay "As We May Think" introduced the **Memex** — a hypothetical personal knowledge device that anticipated the [[concepts/llm-wiki-pattern]] by 80 years.

## The connection to this wiki

Karpathy explicitly cites Bush in the source essay:

> The idea is related in spirit to Vannevar Bush's Memex (1945) — a personal, curated knowledge store with associative trails between documents. Bush's vision was closer to this than to what the web became: private, actively curated, with the connections between documents as valuable as the documents themselves. The part he couldn't solve was who does the maintenance. The LLM handles that. ^[extracted]

Bush identified the right *form* of knowledge organization (private, curated, associative) but couldn't solve the *labor* problem (who maintains the trails). LLMs close that gap. ^[extracted]
